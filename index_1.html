About

I am an Assistant Professor of Chemical Engineering at Howard University's College of Engineering and Architecture, where I joined the faculty in September 2024. My work spans sustainable bioprocess engineering, bioindustrial manufacturing, and biorefinery design, with a focus on developing economically and environmentally defensible pathways from waste streams to value-added products.

I received my Ph.D. in Bioproducts and Biosystems Engineering from the University of Minnesota (2017, advisor: Bo Hu) and my B.Sc. in Industrial Chemical Engineering from the Engineering School of Lorena, University of São Paulo (2014). Before joining Howard, I was a Professor and Director of Research at EARTH University in Costa Rica (2020–2024) and a Postdoctoral Associate at the University of São Paulo.

Research

My lab — the Bioindustrial Manufacturing and Biorefinery Laboratory — works at the intersection of biological and chemical processing with a strong emphasis on techno-economic analysis (TEA) and life-cycle assessment (LCA).

  • Methane mitigation and valorization: methanotrophic systems for low-concentration methane streams, reactor design, gas–liquid mass transfer, and bioprocess modeling.
  • Lignin depolymerization: dirigent-protein-assisted laccase systems for mechanistic lignin bioconversion; downstream bioproduct recovery.
  • Enzyme-enabled processes: laccase immobilization, reactive membranes, ionic liquids, and process intensification.
  • Biorefinery and TEA/LCA: circular bioeconomy systems, waste-to-value platforms, sustainable aviation fuel, and bioindustrial manufacturing.
  • Engineering education: project-based learning, process simulation (DWSIM), sustainability integration, and capstone design.

Teaching

  • CHEG 407 — Process Design I: process synthesis, material and energy balances, safety, economics, and sustainability.
  • CHEG 414 — Process Design II / Capstone: full process design project from concept to preliminary economics, including DWSIM simulation.
