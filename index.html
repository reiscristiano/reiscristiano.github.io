Cristiano Rodrigues Reis

Assistant Professor of Chemical Engineering, Howard University

I lead research and teaching at the intersection of bioprocess engineering, agricultural-residue valorization, enzymatic and microbial processes, techno-economic analysis, and sustainable manufacturing. The Reis Lab develops engineering approaches for turning waste carbon and biomass into fuels, chemicals, materials, and learning opportunities for the next generation of chemical engineers.

Research

Engineering bioprocesses for sustainable carbon and biomass utilization

Methane valorization and methanotrophic systems

We study methane-oxidizing bioprocesses, reactor concepts, and modeling strategies for low-concentration methane emissions, with emphasis on feasibility, environmental relevance, and scalable process design.

methanotrophsCSTR modelinggas fermentation

Biomass-to-bioproducts and biomaterials

We develop pathways to transform agricultural residues and underused biomass into value-added products, including biopolymers, composites, and bio-based materials.

lignocellulosePHAbiocomposites

TEA, LCA, and process design education

We combine process simulation, techno-economic analysis, life-cycle thinking, and green chemistry principles to evaluate technologies and train students to make defensible engineering decisions.

Teaching

Chemical engineering design, simulation, sustainability, and professional formation

CHEG 407 — Process Design I

Early-stage process design, problem definition, process synthesis, material and energy balances, preliminary economics, process safety, and sustainability-informed decision-making.

CHEG 414 — Process Design II / Capstone

Team-based capstone design using process simulation, alternatives analysis, equipment sizing, economics, safety, incentives, and engineering recommendations for real-world technologies.

Opportunities

A clear recruitment section helps students know whether to contact you

The Reis Lab welcomes motivated undergraduate and graduate students interested in bioprocess engineering, sustainable manufacturing, process simulation, TEA/LCA, and biomass valorization. Students with interests in experimental bioprocessing, process modeling, data analysis, and environmental applications are encouraged to reach out.

To inquire about opportunities, send a brief email with your background, research interests, CV or resume, and a short note explaining why the lab is a good fit.
